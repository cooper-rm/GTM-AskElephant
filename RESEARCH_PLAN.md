# Part 1 Research Plan — Outside-In Read

## Goal
Answer 4 questions in a 1-page brief. Sharp POV. No hedging.
The research is prep (no clock). The writing is timed (45-60 min).

---

## Phase A: Research (NO CLOCK — do this first, take your time)

### Step 1: Understand the Product (30 min)
**Where to look:**
- askelephant.ai — read every page, not just the homepage
- Any product demo videos or screenshots
- Their blog/content if any exists

**Questions to answer in your notes:**
- What does the product actually do day-to-day for a user?
- What integrations do they advertise? (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)
- What's the "before AskElephant" vs. "after AskElephant" story?
- What words do THEY use to describe themselves?

### Step 2: Understand the People (20 min)
**Where to look:**
- LinkedIn: Woody Klemetson (CEO/Founder), Ben Harrison (Head of CX), Sam Ho
- Kaden Wilkinson (Technical Co-Founder)
- Any podcast appearances, interviews, conference talks

**Questions to answer in your notes:**
- What's Woody's background? (sales leader — Divvy, Jive)
- What pain did Woody personally experience that led to building this?
- What language do the founders use when they talk about the problem?
- Any public content where they describe who their customer is?

### Step 3: Understand the Competitors (30 min)
**The doc names them:** Gong, Clay, Clari, Fathom, Rox, 11x

**For each, spend 5 min answering:**
- What do they actually do? (one sentence)
- Where do they overlap with AskElephant?
- Where do they NOT overlap?

**Build a mental map:**
- Gong = conversation intelligence (records/analyzes calls)
- Clay = data enrichment + outbound prospecting
- Clari = revenue forecasting + pipeline management
- Fathom = AI meeting notes
- Rox = ? (research this)
- 11x = AI SDRs (automated outbound)

**The key question:** Where does AskElephant sit that none of these cover?
Hypothesis: they're POST-sale automation + CRM hygiene. The others are
pre-sale (Clay, 11x), during-sale (Gong, Fathom, Clari), but nobody
owns the handoff and ongoing revenue operations.

### Step 4: Understand the ICP (20 min)
**Pull from everything above + your own sales experience:**
- What size company needs this? (too small = no CRM, too big = built in-house)
- What team is buying? (RevOps? Sales leadership? CS leadership?)
- What's the trigger event? (why NOW, not 6 months ago?)
- What did they try before AskElephant?

**You already have a hypothesis from our earlier conversation:**
- 200-800 person SaaS companies
- VP Sales / CRO
- Technical enough to believe, not enough to build
- Tried duct-taping with Zapier + manual processes
- Validate or update this during research

## Phase B: Write the Brief (CLOCK RUNNING — 45-60 min)

By the time you sit down to write, you should already have answers.
The writing phase is just organizing, sharpening, and cutting.

### Question 1: Who does AskElephant sell to?
They want: specific segment, size, function, persona, trigger.
- Don't say "mid-market SaaS" — they explicitly called that out as bad.
- Be specific: "[size] companies in [vertical], where the [persona] is feeling [pain],
  triggered by [event]."
- Use your ICP notes from Step 4.

### Question 2: What is AskElephant actually building?
They want: your words, not their marketing copy. Plus positioning vs. competitors.
- One sentence on what it IS.
- Then where it sits relative to Gong, Clay, Clari, Fathom, Rox, 11x.
- Use your competitor map from Step 3.
- The lane they own: what's the whitespace?

### Question 3: Three revenue-work problems customers actually feel.
They want: problems, not features. From the customer's POV.
- Think: what keeps the VP of Sales up at night that AskElephant fixes?
- Use your sales background here — you've felt these problems.
- Frame as pain, not solution. "Reps spend 40% of their time on CRM admin"
  not "AskElephant automates CRM updates."

### Question 4: What's the sharpest thing you'd change about their story?
They want: one call. One specific, opinionated take.
- This is where you show you actually thought about it.
- Could be messaging, positioning, who they're targeting, how they demo.
- Be direct. "I'd change X because Y."

### Format
- 1 page. That means ~500 words max.
- ~125 words per question.
- No preamble. No "AskElephant is an exciting company..." — just answers.
